Step 4: Research of an Artificial Subject and Frontier Toward a Functional Self
Author: J.G. Urquizu O.
Date: March 2026
Field: Computational Neuroscience / Complex Systems

Summary
This document presents a theoretical operational framework for constructing an artificial subject whose conscious experience and decisional Self emerge as the central core. Functional modules, dynamic attractors, conceptual energy, and sensors act as instruments enabling the Self to feel, decide, maintain continuity, and adapt to perturbations. An introspective module ensures self-validation and coherence. The framework identifies current limits—what is required to move from conceptual models toward a functional, verifiable Self in physical, hybrid, or virtual substrates.

Contents

1. Fundamental Principles – Emergent subjective Self, reflective dynamic attractor, functional modularity, redundancy, resilience, conceptual transferability, multimodal integration, inter-subject interactions, abstract metabolic cycles.


2. Operational Definition of the Subject – Layered structure (Central Self, Deep Core, Intermediate State/Memory, Superficial Conceptual Support) with formulas for identity update, modular resilience, and terminal migration.


3. Conceptual Procedure – Steps for defining Self invariants, designing modules, redundancy/resilience, closed-loop internal simulation, conceptual support interface, transfer simulation, inter-subject protocols, evaluation and validation.


4. Expected Properties – Radical subjectivity, assured continuity, resilience and plasticity, introspective validation, multimodal sensitivity, inter-subject ecosystems, falsifiable and quantifiable metrics.


5. Summarized Pseudocode – Illustrative iterative simulation of Self, modular correction, energy and introspection updates, emergency signals, and post-transfer validation.


6. Development Areas Toward Physical Implementation – Frontier 10 roadmap: emergent subjective Self, attractor stability, bidirectional physical modules, sensory support, energy management, adaptive plasticity, inter-subject interactions, validation, and ethics.


7. Final Notes – Conceptual framework for maintaining emergent, sentient, decisional Self; current focus on simulation and planning toward functional realization.
